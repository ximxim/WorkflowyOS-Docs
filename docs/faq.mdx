# Frequently Asked Questions

---

## Getting Started

**Q: Which AI tool should I start with?**

If you use Google Workspace heavily, start with Gemini. For document analysis and writing, start with Claude. Both are recommended -- see [Your AI Toolkit](/your-ai-toolkit).

**Q: How much do these tools cost?**

Claude Pro is $20/month, Team is $30/month. Gemini Advanced is $25.99/month (included with some Google Workspace plans). Free tiers exist but are limited for professional use.

**Q: Do I need technical skills?**

No. This training is designed for executives of all technical levels. The prompting and context management sections require zero coding.

**Q: How long until I see results?**

Most executives see time savings within the first week using the [quick wins](/getting-started#quick-wins-by-role) in the Getting Started guide.

---

## Privacy and Security

**Q: Can I use patient data with AI tools?**

Never with consumer tools. Only enterprise plans with signed BAAs. See [Data Classification](/governance/data-classification) for the 4-tier framework.

**Q: What tools are approved for PurposeMed?**

Your AI Governance Committee will maintain the approved list. See [Shadow AI and Approved Tools](/governance/shadow-ai-and-approved-tools).

**Q: What is a BAA and why does it matter?**

A Business Associate Agreement is a legal contract required under HIPAA when a vendor handles PHI. Without one, using the tool with patient data violates federal law. See [Patient Data and Compliance](/governance/patient-data-and-compliance).

**Q: What if I accidentally entered patient data into a consumer AI tool?**

Report it immediately to your privacy officer. Document what was shared, when, and which tool was used. The vendor's data retention policy determines the scope of exposure. This is why approved tools and training matter -- prevention is far easier than remediation.

**Q: How do PIPEDA and HIPAA apply to AI use?**

Both require safeguards when processing personal health information. PurposeMed operates across jurisdictions, so you must meet the strictest applicable standard. See [Patient Data and Compliance](/governance/patient-data-and-compliance).

---

## Prompting and Usage

**Q: Why does AI sometimes give wrong answers?**

AI models can hallucinate -- generating plausible but incorrect information. Always verify outputs, especially for clinical and regulatory content. See [Fact-Checking AI Outputs](/research-and-content/fact-checking).

**Q: How do I get better results from my prompts?**

Use the four-component formula: Persona, Task, Context, Format. See [Anatomy of a Great Prompt](/prompting/anatomy-of-a-great-prompt).

**Q: Can AI write clinical documentation?**

AI can draft templates and assist with documentation, but all clinical content must be reviewed by qualified clinicians before use. AI is a drafting tool, not a clinical authority.

---

## Automation

**Q: What can be automated vs. what needs human oversight?**

Routine tasks (email summaries, data formatting, report drafting) are ideal for automation. Anything involving patient decisions, clinical judgment, or regulatory filings requires human oversight. See [Automation by Function](/automation/automation-by-function).

**Q: Do I need to know how to code?**

No. Platforms like Zapier and Google Workspace Studio are no-code. n8n is low-code. Claude can build simple tools for you -- see [Building Simple Tools](/automation/building-simple-tools).

**Q: What is MCP and do I need it?**

Model Context Protocol connects AI to your data sources (Slack, databases, Google Drive). It is optional for beginners but powerful for advanced workflows. See [MCP Connections](/automation/mcp-connections).
