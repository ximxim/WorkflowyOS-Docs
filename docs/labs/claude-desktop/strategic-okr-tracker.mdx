# Strategic OKR Tracker

Track PurposeMed's quarterly objectives and key results with health scoring, department-level filtering, and weekly check-in schedules. The tracker surfaces at-risk and off-track OKRs, shows confidence scores, and provides a real-time view of progress across Executive, Clinical Ops, Growth, People & Culture, Engineering, and Finance.

---

## Requirements

The lab brief describes an OKR tracker for Q1 2025 with five strategic objectives spanning AI operations, patient growth, governance, automation, and team culture. It should calculate overall health, track individual KR progress, flag items needing attention, and support weekly check-in workflows by department.

![Strategic OKR Tracker requirements](/img/labs/claude-desktop/strategic-okr-tracker-requirements.png)

---

## Result

The artifact displays a light-themed OKR dashboard with 51% overall health, 5 objectives, and status distribution (6 On Track, 5 At Risk, 5 Off Track). Department tabs filter views across all teams. Each objective shows a confidence score, owner, and KR count. A sidebar panel tracks weekly check-in streaks and highlights the 10 KRs needing attention with progress bars.

## How to Recreate This

1. Define your quarterly objectives and key results with owners and targets
2. Open [Claude](https://claude.ai) and start a new conversation
3. Share your OKR structure and ask for an interactive tracker with health scoring and check-in workflows
4. Iterate -- add historical trends, department rollups, or alignment mapping between objectives

:::tip
OKR trackers work best when you provide the actual objectives, key results, and current progress data. Claude can calculate health scores and surface at-risk items automatically. See [Measuring AI ROI](/leadership/measuring-ai-roi) for frameworks on tracking AI initiative outcomes.
:::
